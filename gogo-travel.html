Go Go Travel

Agent Portal Redesign

Role: Junior Designer

Lead: Edwin Rogers

Tools: Omnigraffle, Photoshop, Illustrator

Project Completed: July 2015

The Background

GOGO Worldwide Vacations is the largest privately owned leisure travel chain in the United States that serves over one million customers annually. They currently have over 170 stores in 14 states.

GOGO Travel's agents operate through a booking engine to quickly secure their clients their dream vacation. Working with Softvoyage, it was our job to re-vamp the entire engine to be clean, responsive, and intuitive.

The Problem

Booking a vacation has a lot of moving parts, and the UI must be clear, concise, and intuitive in order to guide the user. We espeicially wanted to simplify the experience of choosing an airplane seat. The old GoGo travel interface had minimal visuals and no way to directly choose a seat with a clear UI. Airplane seats can be the most expensive part of a vacation, and we wanted to increase user confidence that they were making the right choice.

The User

Lucinda

Senior Agent & Portal Power User

Behaviors

  • constant multi-tasking between the phone and screen
  • meticulous record-keeping on behalf of her clients
  • cherishes her time away from the screen

Goals

  • provide the best service for my client
  • flawlessely book trips quickly and efficiently
  • retain and grow my own relationships with my clients for repeat business

My clients are always asking for a seat with the most leg-room on the airplane, but it’s not always clear what seats those are.

The Pain Point Journey

Lucinda books an amazing trip for her clients

After a long day of packingher clients get to the airport tired and annoyed by traffic.

Lucinda’s clients board the airplane and find that their seats are less than optimal.

Lucinda gets a less-than happy call from her clients after their trip.

The Strategy

Keep it simple, keep it clean.

Choosing a plane seat can be an overwhelming experience for a user. Not only is it an expensive choice, but there are tons of tiny interactions that dictate how you're going to spend hours of your vacation. There were three key points that I wanted to improve upon.

1. Minimize Distraction

Typically in the past this type of interaction would appear in a modal, amplifying clutter on the user's page and in their digital environment. Research has shown that minimizing page content eases user choice and simplifies the experience.

2. Simplify the information.

One clear pain-point of decision making when it comes to choosing a flight seat is definitely cost-factors and upgrades. With accessability in mind, we wante to make it clear what seats cost and if there was additional features to the seat.

3. Make it delightful.

This can be an especially challenging part of booking a trip, so we wanted to increase user delight through soft color and clear interactive touch-points to heighten confidence.

Sketches & Wireframes

We wanted to make the seat the star of the story here, and give the user as much information as we could. This way when this booking is made, there isn't any surprises by the the time the user's clients get on board. They're already aware if they're in the dreaded exmergency exit seats.
