Almanac Metrics

Deliverables: Logo

Timeline: 3 Weeks

Tools: Illustrator

Almanac Metrics is a personalized platform built for urban farmers that gives them a bird's eye view of their harvest and materials. For this project the client wanted something that felt modern and wasn't too overtly rustic. We also wanted to explore adding in visual themes that played up the metrics side of this business, playing with subtle nods to mathematical motifs.

First Concepts

Final Touches & Adding Color

The client was a huge baseball fan and loved the feel of the retro hand lettering. We decided to go in that direction, and incorporate a smaller icon into the lockup as well. With the logo having two parts to it's whole, the icon could sit by itself on marketing collateral and still feel holistic to the Almanac brand.

The color palette was inspired by old imagery of the Farmer's Almanac. I wanted to stand for a dusty red feeling overall, punctuated with the pop color of the soft yellow.

Tele-Tap

Deliverables: Logo, Marketing Collateral, and Website

Timeline: 4 Months

Tools: Paper, Pencil, Illustrator, Photoshop, Wordpress, HTML, CSS

Tele-Tap is a sewer inspection service start-up in Cincinnati, Ohio. This was a super exciting project and the client wanted to build a new brand from the ground up. The client helps manage his father's enterprise sewer service, and wanted to utilize equipment they already owned to start his own residential sewer inspection.

For our the logo the client wanted to leverage an abstracted pipe motif and the green color as a subtle nod to the paint color used by municipalities to indicate sewer lines.

First Concepts

The client was loving the pipe-play but wanted to incorporate a more circular element to be more overt. For the next round I wanted to leverage the more angular pipe shapes, and merge them with a holding shape that tied it together.

Adding Color & Iteration

We loved the last row of iteration best and decided to push forward with the bifurcated pipe motif, marry it with a more circular element, and push color in a way that felt industrial and modern. I wanted to explore how gradients and subtle edge shading could make this feel more masculine and heavy, without being too loud.

Final Concept

I took this mark and developed an identity around it, making sure to keep the green and grays consistent while experimenting with what a brand-pattern of abstracted twisting pipes could do add to the overall system.
